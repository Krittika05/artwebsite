The Painter of Stars

1853 — 1890

Vincent Willem van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who, in just over a decade, created some of the most recognisable artworks in Western history. He produced around 2,100 paintings and drawings, the majority during the final two years of his life, working with a feverish urgency in the wheat fields and starlit skies of Provence.

"I would rather die of passion than of boredom."

Misunderstood in his lifetime and selling only a handful of works, Van Gogh struggled with poverty, illness and mental anguish — yet poured every storm inside him onto canvas. His bold brushwork, vibrating colour and emotional honesty laid the foundations of modern art and continue to shape how we see the world today.
